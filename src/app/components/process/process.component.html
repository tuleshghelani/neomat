Investment Casting Process

Investment casting is a complex process where a molten metal is poured into a ceramic shell to create small, precise metal components.
Its original name "lost wax casting," refers to the original wax pattern the ceramics are formed around, then melted and
replaced with alloys of stainless steel, aluminum, cobalt, carbon steel, brass, bronze, and other metals.

Forging Process

Forging is a manufacturing process that involves shaping metal into a desired shape using compressive forces like hammering, pressing, or rolling.
The process can be performed at different temperatures, including cold, warm, or hot. Forging is used to create parts that are stronger and
more durable than other metalworking processes, and are often used in critical applications
